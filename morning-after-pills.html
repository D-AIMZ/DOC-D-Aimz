What Is Emergency Contraception?

Emergency contraception is a method used to help prevent pregnancy after unprotected sex or contraceptive failure. It is intended for occasional emergency use and should not replace regular contraception.

Important: Emergency contraception does not protect against sexually transmitted infections (STIs).

Key Information

⏰ Time Matters

Emergency contraception is generally most effective when taken as soon as possible after unprotected intercourse.

🩺 Professional Guidance

A pharmacist or healthcare professional can help determine the most appropriate option based on your circumstances.

🔒 Safe and Regulated

Use products obtained from licensed pharmacies and trusted healthcare providers.

📋 Follow-Up Care

If you have concerns about pregnancy, side effects, or your menstrual cycle, seek medical advice promptly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does emergency contraception end an existing pregnancy?

No. Emergency contraception is intended to prevent pregnancy before it occurs and does not terminate an established pregnancy.


Can it be used as regular birth control?

Emergency contraception is designed for occasional emergency use. Regular contraceptive methods are generally more effective for ongoing pregnancy prevention.


Where can I get more information?

Consult a licensed healthcare professional, clinic, or pharmacist for personalized medical advice.
